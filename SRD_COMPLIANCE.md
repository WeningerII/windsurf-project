## 📋 What is an SRD?

A **System Reference Document** is the officially licensed, open-content subset of a game system released under the Open Gaming License (OGL). This project uses **ONLY** SRD content to ensure full legal compliance and avoid proprietary material.

## 🔍 Verification & Enforcement

### Before Adding Any Content
1. **Identify the game system** (D&D 5e, Pathfinder 2e, etc.)
2. **Check the official SRD** for that system
3. **Verify content exists in SRD** - if it's not there, **DO NOT ADD IT**
4. **Document the source** - include SRD reference in code comments
5. **Run validation** - `npm test` to ensure compliance

### Official SRD Resources
- **D&D 5e SRD 5.1**: https://dnd.wizards.com/resources/systems-reference-document
- **D&D 3.5e SRD**: https://www.d20srd.org/
- **Pathfinder 1e SRD**: https://www.d20pfsrd.com/
- **Pathfinder 2e SRD**: https://2e.aonprd.com/

### Red Flags - DO NOT ADD
- ❌ Content from supplement books (Xanathar's, Tasha's, etc.)
- ❌ Content from "Advanced Player's Guide" (unless in SRD)
- ❌ Campaign-specific content
- ❌ Setting-specific mechanics
- ❌ Proprietary names or lore
- ❌ Content not found in official SRD

**Root Cause:** Heritages for uncommon ancestries are not part of the Core Rulebook OGL release.

#### Actions Taken
1. Removed all 82 heritage implementations from 23 uncommon ancestries

5. **When in doubt, verify** against official Paizo OGL documentation
